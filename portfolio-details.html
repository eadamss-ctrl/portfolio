Barbell Connect – Training Companion for Olympic Lifters

A concept app designed to help Olympic lifters find gyms, keep track of training, and stay connected with partners—without needing five different tools and a notes app.

Barbell Connect

A concept mobile app that helps lifters find their fitness community — matching them with gyms, groups, and events based on their goals, preferences, and schedule.

Project Overview

Barbell Connect began with a familiar pain point: lifters hear about gyms, events, and potential partners through a messy mix of DMs, group chats, and word of mouth. There’s no single, calm place to discover where to train, who to train with, and what’s happening nearby.

This is an unshipped student concept created in my Interactive Design coursework. I focused on three core flows: onboarding & personalization, finding gyms & groups, and staying in touch through group chats and events.

The Problem

How might we help lifters:

  • Find gyms that actually match their equipment, distance, and vibe.
  • Connect with small, consistent groups instead of random drop-in partners.
  • Keep their coordination (who, where, when) in one place, without becoming another noisy social feed.

As a concept project, there was no existing user base. I relied on goal-directed design, secondary research, and my own experience lifting to define realistic constraints and scenarios.

My Approach

  • Personas & scenarios for different lifter types (new, intermediate, meet-focused).
  • Paper flows to explore the relationship between home, search, groups, and chat.
  • Digital wireframes for key flows: onboarding, search & filters, group creation, chat.
  • Hi-fi mockups that stayed true to those flows, using a calm yellow + teal palette and soft shapes.

The goal wasn’t to design “every screen,” but to ship a believable slice that could be tested with real lifters later.

What Success Would Look Like

Barbell Connect isn’t live yet, so instead of inventing metrics, I defined clear success signals that could guide future usability tests and MVP experiments.

Goal
Users can find at least one relevant gym or group within their first session.
Goal
Onboarding feels helpful, not tedious — most users complete it instead of skipping.
Goal
Group chats stay focused on meeting up, not endless off-topic noise.
Goal
Lifters feel more consistent because logistics (who / where / when) are clearer.

Onboarding & Personalization

Instead of a basic sign-up, Barbell Connect uses an 11-step onboarding flow to understand a lifter’s context: goals, challenges, equipment, environment, and schedule. These answers power smarter suggestions later.

Flow · Finding a Gym

After onboarding, users land in a simple search experience. Instead of long lists and dense filters, the UI leans on clear cards, a single search bar, and a focused modal for refining results.

Flow · Groups & Members

Groups are the heart of Barbell Connect. The design separates “Your Groups” from those you’ve joined, and makes it easy to inspect members and event details before committing.

Flow · Group Chat & Events

Group chats are intentionally simple: no reactions, stories, or algorithmic feeds — just messages, media, and event cards that keep everyone on the same page.

User Profiles

Profiles provide just enough context to decide if someone might be a good fit to train with — without turning the app into a full social network.

What I’d Do Next

This version of Barbell Connect is a polished concept, but it’s intentionally honest about what hasn’t been validated yet. The next steps would focus on getting this in front of real people.

Usability Testing with Lifters

Run think-aloud sessions with 5–7 lifters to watch them go through onboarding, search for a gym, and join a group. Capture where expectations don’t match the flow.

Refine Matching Logic

Translate onboarding answers into more explicit matching rules, then test whether suggestions feel “creepy accurate” or “random” to users.

Experiment with Social Boundaries

Explore how far to go with profiles and chat features without overwhelming users or turning the app into another social network clone.

Extend to Session Tracking

Bring in basic session logging and PR tracking, using the same calm UI patterns, to see how far a single app can go without becoming bloated.
