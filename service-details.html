How I Work With Teams

A closer look at how I approach research, interaction design, and documentation as a senior Interactive Design student looking for internships and focused sprints.

Process · Collaboration 4 min read

Calm, Structured, and Clear

I’m a senior at Kennesaw State University (Interactive Design, graduating Spring 2026) and I gravitate toward projects where we need to untangle complexity, not add more noise. Whether it’s a class project like Barbell Connect and Flourish or an internship sprint, I work best when we have shared goals and a clear direction for each phase.

What It’s Like to Work With Me

My background mixes UX research, interaction design, and visual systems. On projects like Barbell Connect (lean/agile-inspired group work) and Flourish (goal-directed design), I’ve learned how much smoother everything goes when we align early and keep documentation tidy.

I’m comfortable stepping into different parts of the design process: defining flows, creating UI, building Figma component libraries, or helping clean up the structure of an existing file. I’m also realistic about being a student—my goal is to bring structure, clarity, and effort, not to pretend I’ve shipped a hundred products.

Systems Mindset

I look for patterns across flows, screens, and components so we aren’t redesigning the same decision ten different ways.

Clear Documentation

I keep notes, flows, and Figma pages organized so PMs, engineers, and other designers can quickly understand what’s going on.

User-Focused Details

I care about microcopy, hierarchy, and interaction states—the small decisions that help flows feel calm and confident.

Collaborative Critique

I respond well to feedback, ask questions, and use critique as a way to refine the problem, not just the pixels.

Typical Project Journey

Every team and project is different, but most of my work follows a simple four-phase rhythm. This applies to class projects, small freelance work, and potential internship sprints.

1

Discover & Understand

We align on the problem, constraints, and goals. This might include a quick audit of existing screens, reading prior research, or mapping the current flow.

Light research · context-gathering · success criteria
2

Define Flows & Structure

I translate insights into flows, IA, and rough wireframes. The goal is to make sure the experience hangs together before we invest in polish.

User flows · task scenarios · low-fi wireframes
3

Design & Prototype

I build higher-fidelity UI, components, and prototypes in Figma, keeping accessibility and system consistency in mind.

UI design · component libraries · interactive prototypes
4

Refine & Document

We adjust based on feedback, clean up the file, and prep notes, annotations, or handoff details so the work is ready for development or further testing.

Iteration · documentation · handoff prep
