How to View This Work

Logo & Identity

Book Haven · Logo Explorations

A pair of early logo explorations for a fictional bookstore, “Book Haven.” The first direction focuses on a book-as-shelter metaphor, using simple geometry and a muted palette. The second pushes a more playful approach: a 3D “B” constructed as a book, paired with a light, airy wordmark.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator · Shape builder · Gradients · Type pairing

Vector Illustration

Title Sequence Study · The Lion King

A fan-made Illustrator study recreating key frames from The Lion King title sequence. Each frame was rebuilt with the pen tool and flat shapes, focusing on silhouette, color blocking, and character expression rather than fine detail.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator · Pen tool · Layered color fields · Stylized framing

Vector Tracing Study

Vintage Rolls-Royce · Illustration

A detailed tracing project focused on a vintage Rolls-Royce parked in front of a modern home. The goal was to understand complex forms—curved metal, glass, wheels, and architectural lines— using only vector shapes, while keeping the overall illustration clean and stylized.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator · Pen tool · Complex paths · Layered shadows and highlights

Color & Composition Study

Steven Universe · Reimagined Color Wheel

A reimagined color wheel using characters from Steven Universe arranged around a central gem structure. Each hue family is represented by a different set of characters, combining fan art, character posing, and traditional color theory into one cohesive composition.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator · Character tracing · Color theory · Radial composition

Cover Art

“Good Days” · Alternate Album Cover

An alternate cover concept inspired by SZA’s “Good Days.” The piece focuses on warmth and stillness: a close portrait with beaded braids, soft lighting, and hand-painted textures that echo the reflective, intimate tone of the track.

Tools: Digital painting · Portrait lighting · Typography for music packaging

Poster Redesign

Nightcrawler · Movie Poster Reimagined

A poster redesign for Nightcrawler, centering the protagonist’s eyes framed by torn negative space and intersecting crime scene tape. The layout leans on strong contrast, a limited palette, and a grounded city skyline to reinforce the film’s tension and moral ambiguity.

Tools: Adobe Photoshop · Compositing · Type hierarchy · Texture overlays

Concept Poster

The Asylum · Original Film Poster Concept

A fictional psychological thriller poster exploring the idea that “your mind is the prison.” The design uses a stark black-and-white silhouette, a confined padded cell inset, and restrained typography to create a sense of isolation and internal conflict.

Tools: Adobe Photoshop · Double-exposure style compositing · Tagline and title design

Editorial Layout

DRiVER · Mercedes AMG Cover Story

A feature cover for an automotive magazine highlighting the Mercedes AMG. The design combines bold masthead typography, layered headlines, and supporting detail shots to create a sense of speed and performance while keeping the focal point clearly on the car.

Focus: Hierarchy · Cover lines · Image cropping · Type & brand alignment

Menu Layout

“Since 1999” · Speakeasy-Inspired Drink Menu

A cocktail and drink menu designed with a subtle speakeasy vibe but modern readability. The brief focused on typographic hierarchy, aligning ingredient lists, and organizing multiple categories (wine, beer, spirits, cocktails) into a clear, approachable layout. Final designs were dropped into a table mockup to evaluate legibility in context. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Book Jacket Design

The Hunger Games · Jacket Redesign

A full wrap-around jacket reimagining The Hunger Games for a physical mockup. The design incorporates the District 12 emblem as a central motif, spine typography tuned for shelf visibility, and balanced back-cover copy and author bio to feel consistent with contemporary YA releases.

Focus: Front–spine–back cohesion · Long-form text layout · Visual hierarchy

Mobile UI Concept

Memes.com · Marketplace Styled Like StockX

A playful mobile marketplace for buying and selling reaction memes, modeled after StockX’s “bid / ask” interaction pattern. The concept explores how financial-market language (“Lowest Ask,” “Highest Bid,” “Last Sale”) could be translated into a humorous, meme-driven interface while still feeling structured and familiar.

The flow includes a home feed with categories, searchable browse screens, a detailed meme page with pricing history, and a checkout view that mirrors StockX’s step-by-step purchase experience but with lighter, meme-appropriate tone.

Focus: Card layouts · Mobile hierarchy · Pattern borrowing · Playful yet structured visual design
