Project Overview

Flourish started from a simple but very real problem. People try different products, routines, and styles for months and still feel unsure about what actually helps their hair. Progress is slow, pictures sit in the camera roll, and it is hard to connect cause and effect.

This is an unshipped student concept created during my Interactive Design coursework. The focus was on three main flows: onboarding and hair profile, building and tracking routines, and reviewing progress in a way that feels encouraging, not overwhelming.

The Problem

How might we help people who are on a hair growth or hair health journey:

  • Keep track of wash days, treatments, and protective styles without a messy notes app.
  • Remember which products worked well and which ones caused irritation or breakage.
  • See progress over time in a way that feels real, not just based on one good hair day.
  • Stay consistent with routines without feeling nagged or judged by the app.

There was no existing user base for this concept, so I used goal directed design, light secondary research, and my own lived experience maintaining natural hair to frame realistic scenarios and constraints.

My Approach

  • Personas and scenarios for different hair journeys, like protective style wearers, people working on growth, and people focused on scalp health.
  • Task flows for setting up a routine, logging a wash day, and checking past entries before trying a new product.
  • Wireframes to explore how much detail to ask for without turning every log into homework.
  • High fidelity mockups with a calm, soft visual language that feels like self care instead of a medical chart.

The goal was not to design every possible feature. Instead, I focused on a believable slice that could later be tested with people who are actively tracking their hair or want to start.

What Success Would Look Like

Flourish is not live yet, so there are no real metrics. Instead of making numbers up, I defined a few clear signals that would show if the concept is working once it is tested.

Goal
Most users can set up a basic routine in their first session without help.
Goal
Users can answer "When was my last wash day or treatment" in under 10 seconds.
Goal
People feel more confident about which products and habits are helping or hurting their hair.
Goal
Reminders feel supportive, not nagging, and help people maintain more consistent routines.

Collaboration and Prototype Review

Flourish was created as part of a team based studio project. We split the work across flows and then came back together to align on interaction patterns, visual details, and what the prototype needed to communicate during critique.

Instead of rushing for extra credit, we decided to walk through the prototype together as a team. That meant fixing small issues like alignment, arrows on the challenge cards, and consistency between the quiz screens and the profile while we were all in the same room. It also gave us space to talk honestly about what still felt confusing before presenting it in class.

What I Would Do Next

This version of Flourish is a polished concept, but it is also honest about what has not been validated yet. The next steps focus on getting closer to real hair journeys.

Usability Testing with Real Routines

Run think aloud sessions with 5 to 7 people who already track hair routines in notes or not at all. Watch them onboard, build a routine, and log an entry. Capture where the app feels confusing, heavy, or surprising.

Refine Insights and History

Turn the basic timeline into clearer insights, like patterns in breakage, dryness, or growth length, while still keeping the UI soft and supportive instead of clinical.

Improve Product Logging

Make it easier to log products by scanning or selecting from a library, then use reactions to learn what should be suggested or retired over time.

Explore Light Community Features

Carefully test low pressure ways to let users share routines or progress without turning Flourish into a full social network or comparison heavy space.
